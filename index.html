We’ve developed these guidelines to help build the San Francisco Department of Technology brand. You’ll find instructions for logo use, clear space requirements, color palette, typography, and photography treatment.

Please follow these standards to ensure that the Department of Technology brand is well-represented across media.

Logos
Logos

Our logo includes a symbol and a logotype. These elements are exactly positioned and proportionally fine-tuned. Never use the logotype “San Francisco Department of Technology” without the symbol.

Here are two configurations—vertical and horizontal. Approved logo artwork is available via e-mail request from tech.feedback@sfgov.org

Logo clear space
Logo clear space

Regardless of use, the logo requires a minimum amount of space. This space or margin is based on one half of the large hexagon in the symbol. We call this a SFDT unit. All margins on both the vertical and horizontal logos are equal to one SFDT unit. Maintain the margin between the logo and the edge of a page, visual element, or color field. In general, don’t position anything within the clear space, not typography, not design elements, and definitely not other logos.

Improper logo usage
Improper logo usage

We’ve tried to anticipate all the essential needs for logo usage: color variations, size and scale. That said, we would like you to abide by a few simple rules to help you use the San Francisco Department of Technology logo correctly every time.

Never set on a complex background
Never alter colors or opacity
Never reposition elements
Never add typographic effects
Never on an angle
Never condense or extend

Typography
Typography

We use Open Sans as our primary font for the web—Open Sans Semibold for large headlines, and Open Sans Regular for small headlines and body copy.

For MS Word and Powerpoint, when Open Sans is not available, Calibri is the preferred typeface. It’s a system font available on most computers.
